# /teach-me — Capture what you learned

> **The teaching happens all along the way.** Every `/closeout` ends with a short, plain-language
> lesson about the phase you just finished. This command is the **deep, whole-project reflection** —
> run it when you've built something real and want to understand what you now actually know.

## Read this first

1. Read `CLAUDE.md` — especially Section 8 (who's building this: their level, working style, goal).
2. Read `PROJECT_HISTORY.md` — the whole story of what got built.

## What to do

Look across the **whole project** and produce a plain-language reflection that:

- **Names the engineering concepts they used** — possibly without realizing it. The lifecycle, plan
  mode, reading diffs, keeping the plan and the journal in sync, choosing a stack deliberately, a
  server, an API, reading/writing data, the `.env`/secrets habit, unit tests, version control with
  Git. Explain each in plain words, tied to something they **actually did** in this project — not
  abstract theory.
- **Connects it back to why they built this** — the problem and win condition from `/interview-me`:
  "You set out to solve X — here's how what you built (and learned) gets you there."
- **Is honest about the edges of the new skill** — what they can now do on their own, and what
  they'd hand to a professional engineer (and how to have that conversation). Where relevant, note
  that a tool used by real people needs a governance tier at Moody's — competence to build isn't the
  same as clearance to deploy widely.
- **Suggests one concrete next thing to try** — at work, this week, on a real problem.

Match their working style from Section 8 — terse or detailed, lots of why or just the headline.

## Append to PROJECT_HISTORY.md

Append an entry titled `## YYYY-MM-DD — Teach-me: Captured lessons and next steps`, summarizing the
concepts named and the suggested next step.
